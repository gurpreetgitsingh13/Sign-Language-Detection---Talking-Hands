What is ASL?

ASL, or American Sign Language, is a complete, natural language used primarily by the Deaf and hard-of-hearing communities in the United States and parts of Canada. It has its own unique grammar, syntax, and vocabulary, distinct from English, and is expressed through hand shapes, movements, facial expressions, and body language.
